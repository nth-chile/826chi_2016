Letter by Executive Director

Friends, advocates, believers:

In 2016, our students reminded us, time and time again, that writing is a tool from which we understand one another. A tool for untangling the world.

The accomplishments of this year mark 826CHI’s passage into our second decade. The student pipeline grew. Once young published authors, student alumni returned as volunteers. College essay support expanded, teen programs deepened, and, in 2016, a dream came true: 826CHI hired our first alumni to join staff.

With an extraordinary team actualizing the mission, our new space feels even more welcoming.

And while 826CHI was maturing, the city and country around us was thrust into crisis - from the Illinois state budget impasse and teacher layoffs to tragedies in Charleston, Orlando, and San Bernadino. With the loss of Laquan McDonald just blocks from long-time partner school Curie Metropolitan, our community reinforced the role of 826CHI’s mission at moments of adversity.

"

I want allies to listen. I want marginalized students of color to see themselves in all creative arts. [...] Sharing is scary, but it also connects you with others. Don’t be afraid to erase and start over."
– YERIKA, STUDENT

Elevating young voices through writing and publishing helped 826CHI’s community persist with clarity. We began our first comprehensive strategic planning process, imagining our future through a racial justice lens. During In-Schools and Workshops, Field Trips and Tutoring, students traversed the intersections of their hopes and fears. Volunteers listened and teachers opened their classrooms to partnership. Together, we found common ground in story.

These are the things that sustained - and will continue to strengthen - 826CHI. Thank you for standing with our students.

Warmly,

Kendra Curry-Khanna

Mission Statement

826CHI is a nonprofit organization dedicated to supporting students ages 6 to 18 with their creative and expository writing skills, and to helping teachers inspire their students to write. Our services are structured around the understanding that great leaps in learning can happen with one-on-one attention, and that strong writing skills are fundamental to future success. All of our programs are challenging and enjoyable, and ultimately strengthen each student’s power to express ideas effectively, creatively, confidently, and in their individual voice.

Strategic Planning

Throughout the past year, 826CHI has done a lot to define its values. In order to deepen our impact and understand our role in Chicago's creative and literary education landscape, we embarked on the creation of a three-year plan. The final strategic plan will function as a roadmap for the growth of our impact on the quality of and access to literary arts education in Chicago.

—

The Code

What are the top ten qualities and values that run through all 826CHI programs? If a stranger walked into any of 826CHI’s programs, what are the actions and approaches they would be likely to observe? These questions guided the creation of The Code, 826CHI's signature pedagogy that embodies our approach to learning. It includes the ten tenets of being an 826CHI writing coach, our take on the five-stage writing process, and our version of the story arc. This document binds all of our core programs and responds directly to qualities missing within traditional writing instruction. We use the ‘The Code’ to design, plan, and implement our programs.

—

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

826CHI took part in ongoing conversations with 826 National and Bellwether Education Partners to advance diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives network-wide, which has resulted in the development of policies centered around recruitment, hiring, and leadership development, the creation of affinity groups across the network, and the launch of a comprehensive language audit at our chapter. In addition, 826CHI kicked off a monthly Diversity Series; staff set aside time every month to discuss inequality and equity and their effect on our students and us as educators. Each staff-led session was organized around a question, article, or excerpt that pertains to Chicago students. These discussions have helped affirm our commitment to building diverse and inclusive environments for our students, our volunteers, and our staff.

—

Design Cohort

We routinely engage with local professional artists and authors to support our work. One of the significant advancements during the 2015-2016 school year was the establishment of the 826CHI Design Cohort. The Design Cohort is made up of over 30 local illustrators and graphic designers who offer pro-bono support to a myriad of projects, including 826CHI publications and this very Annual Report. Thanks to the Design Cohort, 826CHI has become more efficient and increased the quality of our publications. High quality design shows our students their stories deserve attention.

After-School Tutoring and Writing

97.06%

OF STUDENTS FEEL CONFIDENT ABOUT FINISHING THEIR HOMEWORK ASSIGNMENTS AT 826CHI

From 3:00pm to 6:00pm on Monday through Thursday, 50 students in grades 1-8 arrive to the 826CHI writing laboratory for homework and writing support. Volunteer tutors worked with students to set daily learning goals, respond to writing prompts, and answer homework questions. At the end of each semester, students select a single story from their writing portfolio, prepare it for publication, and share their writing from our stage at public readings. We also launched the Speakers and Snacks Series. Each semester, we invite four guests to participate in a 45-minute Q&A session with questions developed by our students. The aim of this series is to expose students to a variety of professions in the arts and sciences, held by a diversity of people, to enable them to better visualize opportunities for their own futures.

– STUDENT

Volunteers

"

Much of life for children is being told no. 826CHI is a small space where students are told yes—yes, you can write, yes, you are smart, yes, you can be silly and strange, yes, you can follow your art, yes, you can accomplish anything. That is a space I want to be a part of."
– Bryce P., Volunteer Visual Arts Coordinator

Our work is only possible through the dedication of our highly skilled volunteers. During 2015-2016, we enlisted the services of 302 active volunteers to deliver small group support across all of our programs. Volunteers include everyone from educators and authors to lawyers and accountants, and range in life experiences from recent college graduates to retirees. They are the people who truly activate our mission on an individual basis, serving as both writing coaches and social-emotional mentors. It is only because of these committed, enthusiastic, generous individuals that 826CHI could provide free one-on-one and small-group tutoring to 2,361 Chicago students this school year.
